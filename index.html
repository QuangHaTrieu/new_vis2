Tourism in Australia from 2001 to 2024

Australia’s tourism industry is one of the key economic drivers, contributing dramatically to the GDP and creating numerous jobs. These countries provide not only natural wonders but also cultural experiences. The sector is recovering well after the COVID-19 period. Domestic Tourism accounts for the largest part of this industry, which means that most visitors are Australians.

The area chart and the heatmap show that the number of trips to different states in Australia, in particular, and to the whole of Australia, in general, has been increasing over time. The area chart clearly shows a significant drop around 2019 to 2022, caused by the covid and social isolation. However, after COVID, domestic tourists started to revisit different places in Australia, it is demonstrated by the local upward trend from 2022 until now.

The Donut chart illustrates that “Friends or relatives property” and “Hotels and similar” are always the most popular accommodation choices. While there is a more even distribution among different types of accommodation in regions, there is a distinctive difference between different accommodation types in capital cities such as Melbourne and Sydney.

Where do domestic visitors visit the most?

The map shows that most trips come to coastal regions, with the eastern and southern areas being the most popular destinations. In detail, among Australia’s states, Victoria and New South Wales stand out as the most tourist attraction locations, reflecting the attractiveness of major cities like Melbourne and Sydney. Coastal Queensland and a part of Western Australia also attract a moderate number of tourists, but it is less than in southeast regions. In contrast, as we move inland, central Australia and the Northern Territory attract much fewer trips, reflecting these regions' unpopularity as domestic tourism destinations. .

Why Australian residents visit these regions

In 2024, most domestic tourists visit different regions in Australia to visit friends and relatives. The number of trips for this purpose varies significantly among various states. The parallel plot shows that the number of trips for this purpose is highest in New South Wales, followed by Victoria. On the contrary, Business trips consistently have the lowest number among all regions, reflecting that the most common reasons for domestic travel are leisure and social.
